Rosemary Chicken and Orzo

Description

Rosemary chicken thighs with burrata, sundried tomatoes, and orzo. Creamy, flavorful, and incredibly easy, this is a one-pot meal that is sure to impress!

Ingredients

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp salt + 1/2 tsp pepper
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 yellow onion, diced
  • 1 shallot, diced
  • 5 garlic cloves, minced
  • 8 oz sundried tomatoes
  • 2 cups orzo
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 2/3 cup grated parmesan cheese
  • 1 ball burrata cheese
  • salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 475 degrees.
  2. Make the chicken rub. Mix together the dried rosemary and garlic powder into a small bowl. Add olive oil.
  3. Season both sides of the chicken thighs with salt and pepper. Brush both sides with the rub mixture.
  4. Heat a dutch oven over medium high heat. Add butter and a splash of olive oil.
  5. Sear chicken until golden brown. Flip and brown the other side. The chicken should be mostly cooked, about 12 minutes.
  6. Remove chicken and set aside on a dish. Cover the dish with tin foil. Drain some of the excess fat from the dutch oven.
  7. Roughly chop the sun dried tomatoes. Save 1 tbsp of oil from the jar.
  8. Add the sun dried tomato oil to the pan. When it's hot, add the onion and shallot and saute until transluscent.
  9. Add the tomatoes and the garlic, saute until fragrant.
  10. Add the orzo and stir to evenly coat it.
  11. Add the white wine to deglaze the pan. Reduce the white wine while scraping the bottom of the pan for about 1 minute.
  12. Add the chicken stock and stir.
  13. Add the cooked chicken back to the pot and push it down until the liquid covers it.
  14. Reduce heat to medium and simmer for about 15 minutes, stirring frequently, until orzo is cooked and chicken temperature reaches 165 degrees.
  15. Add both cheeses and put the pan into the oven uncovered. Cook until cheese is melted. Serve!
